DRONES [2019-2020]

SURE's primary goal is to identify, design and develop (up to TRL 4) on-board avionics and GNC capabilities for autonomous, safe and robust atmospheric flights.

My main contributions to the project was identifying the need of, and developing, a safety filter for an on-board GNC system. This capability allowed the reduction of dispersions of extreme cases, enforced safety-related constraints and facilitated their monitoring for flight termination.

MARIOT WEBSITE 🡥

Design, construction and testing of an automated flight termination system for big drones (>7kg). This includes the implementation of neural-networks for 1) classification of landable ground areas and 2) structural vibration monitoring; and 3) the formulation of a decision function to terminate the flight. Airborne tests were conducted, as well as lab tests using VICON motion capture system on a DJI M800 to gather nominal and faulty vibration data.

SAMPLE PATH LARGE DEVIATIONS FOR SPACECRAFT COLLISIONS

Freidlin-Wentzell theory of Large Deviations is applied to the problem of spacecraft collisions, a rare event characterized by extremely low probabilities. We accommodate stochastic uncertainty in the initial states, which is a relevant source of uncertainty in this type of problems, and define an optimal control problem which solution are the most likely sample paths, initial conditions and terminal time leading to the realization of the unsafe rare event.

IMPULSIVE MANEUVER DETERMINATION

Maneuver determination is crucial aspect of Space Domain Awareness (SDA). Specifically, we focus on estimating non-cooperative impulsive spacecraft maneuvers in terms of execution time, direction, and magnitude using a multiple-model estimation approach. This technique improves the understanding of the current state of space objects, particularly, but not exclusively, in near-Earth regions, and helps predicting their future trajectories. Enhancing SDA is crucial for making more informed decisions, thereby increasing the overall safety of the space environment.

DESCENTRALIZED GUIDANCE FOR HIGH-PRECISION POINTING

We designed a guidance scheme for the pointing acquisition phase of the Laser Interferometer Space Antenna (LISA) mission. The problem is formulated as a cooperative game and we solve it using a modified gradient-bandit algorithm. The algorithm yields a reference signal for the controllers to align all the spacecraft' laser beams simultaneously within the tolerances required for gravitational wave detection.
